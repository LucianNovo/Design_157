Creative Brief
An overview

Project Summary:
      A highly immersive visual that represents the university’s energy use as a turbulent solar
 system. The visual analogy suggests our utilities are forceful energy bearing masses. 

Audience Profile:
       internet wanderers, students, people with smartphones and technology comprehension, citizens,
children, bloggers, those easily entertained, those more inquisitive.
Perception/positioning:
      an augmented perspective on energy consumption
Communication Strategy:
       Strong visuals and haptics, data responsive visuals.
Look and Feel:
      Other than the tron-inspired visuals, the typographic components of my page will be in source
sans to highlight the data influences
Targeted Feel:
      Your university is as chaotic as the solar system.
Needfinding
Understanding user values, interests, and feelings.
I introduced the MacPro website to a friend and observed their interaction

The interaction gist
       As the user scrolls through the website the various technical innards of the product
reveal themselves. The product rotates and moves to give the user a physical understaning. 

User Experience:
       There's a clear flow through the special-effects features of website.  The user only has to scroll
to glide between animations. In terms of immersion, the animations do the heavy lifting. With the 
the stealthiest movement, the components can fly out of place or the entire module can spin.  As high-
lighted by my user, absolutely no clicking was required, and multiple scroll directions were accep-
ted. Although this was the most ergonomic way to interact with the website, another visual form
was provided. This secondary method distracted from the intended haptic method of interacting.
When the user discovered the scrolling-based navigation system they revisited the entire site's 
interaction. 
Takeaways:
  • The user is not there to use the interface, but feel delighted by it.
  • A secondary navigation system is a distraction
  • A well done effect is revisited
  • Present everything in a sequential order, make a procession/story
